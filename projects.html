College

UMD Events

For my honors college final project, I created a web application in Ruby on Rails which allows students to view events happening around campus. The application included user sign up, email reminders, tags and searching. When a user is signed in, they can save events they would like to go to, and even download their saved events to a personal calendar. Events are entered by users and moderated by admins.

NewsStand

I worked with a professor and grad students on a NSF grant supported multi-platform system. The system read in news data from thousands of RSS newsfeeds feeds and used various algorithms to display/group the stories on a map based interface. I worked primarily with Ruby, JavaScript and the Google Maps API to develop features and fix bugs

QR Scavenger Hunt

A webapp created with PHP and MySql which used physical QR codes placed around campus to create an interactive scavenger hunt. Students used their smartphones to find QR codes placed around campus then entered them into the application to reveal the location of the next one. The application included user signup and a leaderboard.

National Honor Society Application Webapp

Me and my partner were drawn to our high schools outdated NHS application process through our computer science professor. Teachers, evaluators and students were completing the application process using hundreds of pieces of paper that needed to be passed around to multiple people. We developed this application in PHP using a MySQL datastore to replace the full application and review process. It is still in use today.

Microsoft Surface Applications

During my junior and senior year of highschool, our school got involved with a special program with Microsoft where we got an original Microsoft Surface touch-screen table. Along with 2 of my friends, we started a club at school where we developed a Surface applications. The apps were programmed mostly in C# and WPF and by the end of our senior year we had produced two larger scale applications: one for teacher conferencing and an educational geography game. Check them out on our YouTube channel!
